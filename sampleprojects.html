NASA's Perseverance Rover

NASA's Perseverance Rover started being developed in 2013. In 2020, it landed in the Jezero Crater on Mars and has been exploring the planet ever since. There are a couple of things that set Perseverance apart from other rovers. For one, it was sent to Mars with the express purpose of checking for signs of water and ancient life. Additionally, it has a couple of unique abilities. The first is that it can drill samples of the rock on Mars, which will be picked up at a later date. The second is that it has a drone named "Ingenuity" that traveled to Mars with Perseverance, and it uses the rover to navigate the Martian surface and to relay data.

The First Photo of a Black Hole

In 2019, the Event Horizon Telescope, which is actually an international system of radio telescopes, released an image of the black hole at the center of the M87 galaxy, which is relatively close to the Milky Way, at around 55 million light-years away. The diameter of M87's event horizon (the orange disk of matter) is a little over 24 billion miles long, or around 1500 times the width of the solar system. The photo was generated by combining data from 8 different telescopes around the world.
